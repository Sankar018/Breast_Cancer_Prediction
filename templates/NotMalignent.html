Most non-malignant (benign) tumors are not dangerous and do not turn into cancer. They usually grow slowly and don’t spread to other parts of the body. However, some may grow larger or cause discomfort, depending on their location. In rare cases, certain types of benign tumors can change over time, so it’s important to keep an eye on them. Regular check-ups and scans help your doctor make sure everything stays safe. If there’s any sign of change, your doctor will guide you on what to do. So while the tumor is likely not harmful, staying alert is still important.

If you have a non-malignant tumor, follow your doctor’s advice closely. Go for regular check-ups to make sure the tumor doesn’t grow or change. If the doctor suggests surgery, it’s usually safe and can prevent future problems. Eat healthy, stay active, and avoid smoking or alcohol to support your overall health. Watch for any new symptoms like pain, swelling, or changes in the lump. Let your doctor know if anything feels different. Most benign tumors are not dangerous, but staying alert and taking care of your body will help you stay safe now and in the future.
