Healthy Diet Recommendations

Maintaining a balanced diet can support breast health. Consider incorporating the following into your meals:

  • Fruits and vegetables: Aim for at least five servings daily, focusing on dark, leafy greens and berries.
  • Whole grains: Include foods rich in fiber, such as whole grains, beans, and legumes.
  • Low-fat dairy products: Opt for low-fat milk and dairy products.
  • Soy products: Incorporate soybean-based products into your diet.
  • Vitamin-rich foods: Consume foods rich in vitamin D and other essential vitamins.
  • Anti-inflammatory foods: Use spices with anti-inflammatory properties, such as turmeric.
  • Antioxidant-rich foods: Focus on plant-based foods that contain antioxidants.

When to Consult a Doctor or Consider an MRI

While regular self-examination are crucial, consult a healthcare professional if you notice any of the following:

  • A new lump or mass in the breast.
  • Swelling of all or part of the breast.
  • Skin irritation or dimpling.
  • Nipple pain or the nipple turning inward.
  • Redness, scaliness, or thickening of the nipple or breast skin.
  • Nipple discharge other than breast milk.

Early detection is key to effective treatment. If in doubt, always seek medical advice.
