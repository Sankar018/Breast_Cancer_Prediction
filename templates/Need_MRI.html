Why an MRI is Necessary

Based on your selected symptoms, it is important to undergo an MRI as soon as possible.

MRI is important when more than one symptom appears because it gives a clearer and more detailed view of the breast tissues. It helps in detecting tumors that may not be visible in a regular mammogram or ultrasound.

If multiple symptoms like lumps, nipple discharge, or skin changes occur, an MRI can help doctors confirm whether the changes are cancerous or not. This early and accurate detection can lead to timely treatment and better outcomes.

Precautions Before MRI

  • Avoid caffeine for 24 hours before the scan.
  • Remove any metal objects or jewelry.
  • Inform your doctor if you have implants or medical devices.
  • Follow fasting instructions if provided by your doctor.
